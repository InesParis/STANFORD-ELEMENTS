Module Overview

Introduction

There is a transformation taking place in organizations across the globe as they implement generative artificial intelligence (AI) tools into their operations. This transformation is likely to affect nearly every process and structure throughout these organizations, as teams and individuals learn to incorporate algorithmic capability into their workflows. Ensuring that the greatest benefits are realized, leaders and decision-makers play a key role in shaping this algorithmic adoption.

Welcome to Digital Leadership in the AI Age. Throughout this course, Professor Melissa Valentine will explore AI's growing capabilities, workflow implementation, and steps for preparing an organization for AI adoption. We will examine relevant case studies and see how actors from a variety of industries have successfully—and in some cases unsuccessfully—approached the questions surrounding AI and how they have carried out its implementation.

Before we explore specific algorithmic applications, we will consider the essential role of leadership throughout the implementation process. In Module 1, we will examine change management processes in relation to machine learning implementation and examine how some businesses have strengthened their value propositions through the successful use of AI.

Learning Objectives

Describe leaders’ activities that create the conditions for effective implementation of predictive analytics tools and how they shape team members’ adoption and use of these tools

Evaluate existing frames, structures, and evaluation schemes for predictive analytics

Create new framing, create a new proposal for structuring for AI, and define new evaluation processes for AI
